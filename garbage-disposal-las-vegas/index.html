Signs Your Disposal Needs Replacing

Disposals don't last forever, and Las Vegas hard water speeds up the wear. Here's what tells you it's time for a new one:

  • It hums but doesn't spin — motor is seized or jammed beyond a simple reset
  • Persistent leaks at the sink flange, side discharge, or drain connections
  • Loud grinding, rattling, or screeching noises during operation
  • Frequent clogging even with normal food waste
  • It's more than 8–10 years old and showing its age
  • You've reset it multiple times and it keeps failing

What the Installation Involves

A disposal swap isn't just unscrew-and-plug-in. There's a mount system, a drain connection, a dishwasher knockout (if needed), and an electrical connection. We handle all of it:

  • Disconnect and remove the old unit and mounting hardware
  • Install new mounting ring and sink flange — reseat with fresh plumber's putty
  • Connect the new disposal, align drain and discharge properly
  • Connect dishwasher drain line if applicable
  • Check and tighten all connections before powering on
  • Test with water running — check for leaks, confirm proper operation
  • Clean up under the sink completely
