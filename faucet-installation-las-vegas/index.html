Hard Water Is Hard on Faucets

Las Vegas has some of the hardest water in the country. The mineral buildup from Lake Mead water corrodes washers, clogs aerators, and works its way into cartridges and valve seats. A faucet that drips or runs weak isn't just annoying — in Las Vegas it means you're paying for water you're not using, and the situation gets worse over time, not better.

Sometimes a cartridge swap fixes a drip. Often the faucet itself is past its useful life and replacement is the smarter move. We'll give you an honest assessment and a price before we touch anything.

How We Handle the Job

  1. We shut off the water supply and confirm it's off before anything is disconnected.
  2. We remove the old faucet and inspect the supply lines and shut-offs while we're in there.
  3. We install the new faucet per the manufacturer's instructions — no guessing, no shortcuts.
  4. We connect the supply lines, check every connection for leaks, and run the water.
  5. We clear the aerator of any debris from installation, check flow and pressure, and clean up under the sink.
